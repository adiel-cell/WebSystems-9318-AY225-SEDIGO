About Our College

Pioneering computing education since 1995, preparing students for leadership in technology fields.

Our History Mission & Vision

Our History

The College of Computer Studies was established in 1995 with a vision to create a center of excellence in computing education. What began as a small department with just two faculty members and 50 students has grown into one of the region's leading institutions for computer science and information technology education.

Over the years, we have continually evolved our curriculum to keep pace with technological advancements, adding programs in cybersecurity, data science, and IT management to our original computer science offering.

Today, we serve over 1,200 students across undergraduate and graduate programs, supported by more than 45 full-time faculty members and state-of-the-art facilities.

Mission & Vision

Our Mission

To provide exceptional computing education through innovative teaching, cutting-edge research, and industry collaboration, preparing students to become ethical leaders and problem-solvers in a technology-driven world.

Our Vision

To be recognized as a premier institution for computing education and research, known for producing graduates who drive innovation and positively impact society through technology.

Our Values

Innovation

We foster creativity and encourage novel approaches to solving complex problems.

Collaboration

We believe in the power of teamwork and interdisciplinary cooperation.

Excellence

We maintain high standards in teaching, research, and student development.

Integrity

We uphold ethical principles in all our academic and professional endeavors.

Our Facilities

Our college is housed in the state-of-the-art Technology Center, which features:

  • 10 specialized computer laboratories
  • Cybersecurity lab with isolated network for ethical hacking exercises
  • Data science lab with high-performance computing resources
  • Innovation hub for student projects and startups
  • Collaborative workspaces and study areas
  • Research centers in AI, cybersecurity, and human-computer interaction

Our facilities are regularly updated with the latest hardware and software to ensure students have access to industry-standard tools.
